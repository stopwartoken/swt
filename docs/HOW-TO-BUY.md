# How to Buy SWT

A practical guide to interacting with STOPWARTOKEN (SWT) on Polygon.

This document explains the underlying process.
It is not financial advice.

---

## Where to start

The easiest and safest way to interact with SWT is through the official interface:

https://stopwartoken.org/how-to-buy.html

It provides:

- verified contract reference  
- direct links to DEX interfaces  
- safety checklist  
- real-time price and liquidity context  

This document focuses on how the process works.

---

## Requirements

Before swapping, make sure you have:

1. **Wallet**
   - MetaMask, Brave Wallet, or any EVM-compatible wallet

2. **Network**
   - Polygon (chain id 137)

3. **Gas**
   - A small amount of POL for transaction fees

4. **Swap token**
   - USDT / USDC or another token to swap from

## Where to trade

SWT is available on decentralized exchanges (DEX):

- Uniswap V3 (primary)
- QuickSwap (secondary)

For verified access and prefilled swap links:

https://stopwartoken.org/how-to-buy.html

---

## Swap process 

1. Open a DEX interface (Uniswap or QuickSwap)
2. Connect your wallet
3. Set:
   - **From**: USDT / USDC (or other token)
   - **To**: SWT (by contract address)
4. Enter amount
5. Review:
   - price impact  
   - output amount  
   - network (Polygon)  
6. Confirm the transaction

---

## Notes on pricing

- Pricing depends on liquidity in active ranges (Uniswap V3 / Algebra)
- Quotes may differ between Uniswap and QuickSwap
- Larger swaps increase price impact
- Real execution may differ slightly from UI estimates

---

## Fees

- SWT includes a **0.1% on-chain humanitarian fee**
- DEX swap fees also apply (depending on pool)

---

## Safety checklist

Before confirming a transaction:

- Verify the **contract address**
- Confirm **Polygon network**
- Check **token symbol and decimals**
- Review the **destination token in wallet UI**
- Avoid links from DMs or third-party reposts

---

## Why start small

A small first swap helps confirm:

- correct token  
- correct pool  
- correct wallet behavior  

This is a safety step, not an investment strategy.
